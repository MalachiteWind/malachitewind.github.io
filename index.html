About

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate in Applied Mathematics at the University of Washington, advised by Dr. J. Nathan Kutz. I work on creating reduced order models for characterizing plume dynamics, as well as optimization methods for model discovery from sparse data observations.

Outside of work I am an avid rock climber. I also enjoy solving rubiks cubes where I regularly compete in competitions in the pacific northwest.
